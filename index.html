IMPACTO (Impact Assessment Ontology) is an ontology for representing impact assessment requirements and processes based on the GDPR and the EU AI Act. IMPACTO specifically represents the Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) and the Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA), assisting stakeholders in determining when assessments are required, structuring assessment processes, and representing concepts such as processing contexts, risks, impacts, locations, personal data categories, and AI capabilities relevant to impact assessments across EU Member State jurisdictions.

Introduction

The GDPR (Art. 35) and the EU AI Act (Art. 27) impose obligations on controllers and providers of high-risk AI systems to conduct impact assessments before beginning processing activities/ deploying AI systems that are likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. These obligations require organisations to identify whether their processing activities or AI systems are high-risk and therefore require an assessment, then to document risks and impacts, and implement technical and organisational measures to mitigate identified risks.

Across EU Member States, Data Protection Authorities (DPAs) have published jurisdiction-specific lists of processing activities that require ("blacklists") or do not require a DPIA ("whitelists"). Navigating these lists in a consistent, interoperable, and machine-readable way is a significant challenge for organisations operating across multiple jurisdictions. Especially due to the fact that less than one third are available in English. Similarly, the EU AI Act introduces the Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA) for deployers of high-risk AI systems used in certain public and private sector contexts, adding further complexity to the compliance landscape.

IMPACTO proposes an ontology-based approach to represent the concepts and relations required to support both the DPIA and the FRIA processes. It builds on and extends established vocabularies — primarily the Data Privacy Vocabulary (DPV) and its extensions — and proposes additions to DPV, location, personal data, risk, technology, AI, and sector-specific modules to fill gaps identified through analysis of the GDPR, the EU AI Act, and Member State DPA guidance.

IMPACTO is an ontology expressed using semantic web technologies that represents impact assessment information in a formal, machine-readable, and interoperable manner. It assists stakeholders in determining whether a DPIA or FRIA is required, structuring the content and process of these assessments, and sharing assessment information across jurisdictions and systems.

Overview

Core Concepts and Relations

IMPACTO's concepts are organised into groups corresponding to the DPV modules they extend or complement: core DPIA process concepts, processing context concepts, location concepts, personal data categories, risk and impact concepts, technology and AI concepts, and sector-specific concepts. The ontology reuses and extends DPV properties such as dpv:hasRisk, dpv:hasConsequence, dpv:hasPersonalData, dpv:hasJurisdiction, and dpv:hasPurpose to link these concepts together in impact assessment representations.
